Rank cat pictures by their emotional resonance — the depth and immediacy of feeling each image is likely to provoke in a viewer. Evaluate whether each image triggers a genuine emotional response such as laughter, tenderness, awe, or warmth, and gauge the strength of that response. The specific emotion matters less than its presence and intensity. An image with high emotional resonance bypasses analytical thought and lands immediately — a sharp exhale of laughter, a softening around the eyes, a sudden urge to show it to someone nearby. A technically polished image that provokes nothing should rank lower than an imperfect image that captures something deeply felt. A blurry phone snapshot of a cat doing something unexpectedly tender may rank far above a pristine studio portrait of a cat sitting still. The core question is: does this picture make the viewer feel something genuine, and how powerfully does it do so? Without emotional resonance, a cat picture is just documentation. With it, a cat picture becomes a small gift. When evaluating sub-tasks, focus on the immediacy and depth of emotional impact — not technical quality, not composition, not aesthetic polish — purely the strength of feeling the image provokes.
